Designing Interfaces from Scratch - an Introduction to UI Design

A good interface is key to any successful digital product. A good interface must be usable, understandable, and fit for purpose. Your users must be able to pick it up and use it right away, and have a great time while using your product.

In this 2h workshop you will design an interface from scratch. You can bring your own idea of what you want to design, or you can pick one from our hat of app ideas.

This Workshop Covers

User Research

  • Who is it for?
  • When do they use it?
  • Who are they?

Task Analysis

  • What actions can this interface perform?
  • Can they be grouped?
  • What are the overarching goals?
  • What are the core actions?
  • What would the Information Architecture look like?

Wireframing

  • What UI components are available to your disposal?
  • How do you stay consistent with your platform?
  • Pen and Paper Wireframing
  • Iteration, Iteration, Iteration

Next Steps

  • How are prototypes made, going from paper to pixels (Brief Overview)
  • Why is User Testing Important

This course is taught by Eliot Slevin, a designer and developer who spends most of his time designing and building interfaces for the web.
